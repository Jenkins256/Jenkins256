Color Symphonies

Copeland Gallery, London, August 2022

The work explores what can be defined as a photograph in the modern age. The photographs I create are not intended to be understood but to be felt, experienced and seen.

The processes aim to create discord within the traditional photographic processes. I take inspiration from previous artistic movements and most importantly jazz, to create a method of spontaneity directly related to the materials which result in unpredictable images that are dislocated from the tangible world, responding to the environment in which they are displayed.

Striking Artistry

Thomas Jenkins (b. 1992) is a visual artist working with photographic processes. His work explores colour and abstraction to question what is defined as a photograph in the modern day. The methods stem from interests in contemporary and historical photography, painting and sculpture.

Teaching

Thomas has worked at North Herts College supplying a high quality of teaching for several years. He also has an MA in Photography from the Royal College of Art and a BA Hons in Digital Film and Screen Art from UCA Farnham.

Photography

Tom has always had a passion for photography and can provide professional photography services for corporate or private events.
